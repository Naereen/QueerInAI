Queer in AI@NIPS

Queer in AI is hosting a social and satellite workshop at NIPS, featuring several speakers, a panel on ethics, AI/ML, and queer issues, and a poster session highlighting the research contributions of queer scientists.

Social
The Queer in AI@NIPS social will be held at Bar le Cocktail from 7pm-10pm.

Workshop Schedule
The workshop will be hosted in the S. Bernhardt room and the poster session in the adjacent C. Sherwood S./C.S. Stratton room of the Intercontinental Montreal, located across the street from the main NIPS venue.

No advance submission is nessecary for the poster session. Please bring your poster to the C. Sherwood S./C.S. Stratton room in the Intercontinental Montreal between 8 and 8:30am Saturday morning to set up.

Panel Abstract
Machine learning is widely used to categorize people and has rapidly increasing influence over the world. Queer people do not fit into traditional categories. This raises several issues about how machine learning interacts with queer people. Machine learning algorithms trained on data that is not inclusive of queer people may misclassify them. In addition, unwanted or accidental inference/disclosure of a queer personâ€™s identity can be devastating to their well-being, physical, legal, social, or otherwise. We propose a panel to raise awareness of these issues and increase the visibility of queer people in the NIPS community. Specifically, we will host relevant experts and decision-makers from industry and academia to discuss how ML algorithms and datasets may be made more inclusive of queer people, how to protect data which may be used to identify queer people, and other challenges and potential benefits of ML/AI for queer people.
